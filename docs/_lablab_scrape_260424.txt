Agentic Economy on Arc
The Agent Economy Built for Sub-Cent Transactions
In this hybrid hackathon powered by Arc and Circle , developers will build the next generation of Agentic economic applications using Circle’s Nanopayments infrastructure with settlement on Arc in USDC.

Arc provides stablecoin-native settlement with predictable, dollar-denominated USDC fees and deterministic, sub-second finality — enabling real-time coordination and economically viable per-action pricing at scale.

About
Circle (NYSE: CRCL) is a global financial technology firm that enables businesses of all sizes to harness the power of digital currencies and public blockchains for payments, commerce, and financial applications worldwide. Circle is building the world’s largest, most-widely used, stablecoin network, and issues, through its regulated affiliates, USDC and EURC stablecoins.
Arc is the purpose-built L1 blockchain from Circle and serves as the Economic OS for the internet — powering the programmable trust layer and transaction engine where capital, humans, and machines coordinate to drive real economic growth.
Together, Nanopayments and Arc unlock a new economic primitive for the internet: programmable value that is finally viable at high frequency.
Key benefits for developers:
• Process sub-cent transactions on-chain, gas-free , with full control of funds
• Align pricing precisely with usage — no margin lost to gas fees
• Charge per API call, per query, per millisecond of compute, or per action
Agentic Economy in action:
• APIs monetize every request
• AI agents pay for data and services in real time
• Compute marketplaces priced by usage
• Machine-to-machine commerce without intermediaries
The internet made information programmable.
Circle Nanopayments + Arc make value programmable — and economically viable.
Challenge
Build the Agentic Economy on Arc using USDC and Nanopayments.
Your mission is to design and ship an application, agent, or developer tool that leverages programmable USDC and Nanopayments for economically viable transactions to unlock new economic models for APIs, AI Agents, marketplaces, or machine-to-machine commerce.
You’ll choose one of the tracks below (or naturally align to one) and submit a working demo plus a clear explanation of your use case and feedback .
You must align your project with at least one track. Winners are selected based on the best overall solutions, regardless of track.
All submissions must :
• Demonstrate real per-action pricing (≤ $0.01)
• Show transaction frequency data (at least 50+ onchain transactions in demo)
• Include a margin explanation: why this model would fail with traditional gas costs
This forces economic proof, not just a demo.
Tracks:
🪙 Per-API Monetization Engine
Build an API that charges per request using USDC, demonstrating viable per-call pricing at high frequency.
🤖 Agent-to-Agent Payment Loop
Create autonomous agents that pay and receive value in real time, proving machine-to-machine commerce without batching or custodial control.
🧮 Usage-Based Compute Billing
Design an application that charges per query, per compute unit, or per task with real-time settlement aligned to actual usage.
🛒 Real-Time Micro-Commerce Flow
Build a buyer or seller experience where economic activity is triggered and settled per interaction, not per subscription.
💡 Product Feedback Incentive
We’re offering $500 USDC in rewards to the teams that provide the most detailed and helpful feedback. To be eligible, teams must complete the feedback field as part of their final project submission .

Tracks
Choose one - or mix and match. Each track explores a core use case of programmable payments in the age of agentic systems:
🤖 Agent-to-Agent Payments
Challenge : Build a system where two or more agents autonomously trigger and settle payments—e.g., for usage-based services, access control, or dynamic pricing. Your product should demonstrate a working agentic payment loop with minimal human input.
🧑‍💼Consumer AI Payments
Challenge : Develop an AI assistant that can make payments on a user’s behalf—with built-in rules like spending limits, approval checkpoints, or identity verification (KYC/AML). Showcase how your product handles decision-making, compliance, and safeguards.
🏢 B2B FinOps & Compliance
Challenge : Build a tool for businesses to manage real-time payments and financial operations—like tracking cash flow, enforcing policies, or generating audit-ready records. Highlight automation, accuracy, and visibility into payment activity.
🛒 On-chain Commerce Primitives
Challenge : Launch a digital product or service with a built-in revenue model using X402. Think: token-gated access, real-time rev-splits, or instant payouts to contributors. Your prototype should demonstrate seamless, trustless commerce flows.

Technology & Access
To build your project, you will use Arc (settlement layer), USDC (value), and Circle developer infrastructure such as Gateway , Nanopayments , and x402 (web-native payment standard) where available.
🔒 Required Technologies :
• Arc - All transactions settle on Arc, an EVM-compatible Layer-1.
• USDC - USDC is the native gas token and stablecoin on Arc and is used for gas fees, transfers, and smart contract interactions.
• Circle Nanopayments - Infrastructure enabling economically viable sub-cent, high-frequency transactions.
🔒 Additional Recommended Technologies :
• Circle Wallets - Recommended core wallet solution for this hackathon.
Use Circle Wallets as the primary wallet infrastructure in your submission to create secure, programmable wallets for users and agents.
• Circle Gateway for a unified USDC balance instantly accessible crosschain
• Circle Bridge Kit for moving USDC across chains

Technology partners
Gemini is Google’s family of next-generation multimodal AI models designed for agent-driven commerce, capable of reasoning across text, images, code, video, and audio. Gemini also supports Function Calling , allowing agents to securely interact with Circle APIs and smart contracts as part of real-world payment and settlement workflows.
Google AI Studio is a browser-based environment where developers and creators can easily explore and prototype with Gemini models - test prompts, refine outputs, and experiment with different modalities before integrating them into real-world applications through the Gemini API .
Challenge :
Build with Gemini models and Google AI Studio to power the intelligence and interface of your agents, then run them on Arc with USDC for agent-driven commerce.
Your project should:
• Use Gemini (via Google AI Studio or Gemini API) for reasoning, chat, or multimodal understanding, and Function Calling , enabling agents to interact directly with Circle APIs and smart contracts .
• Run agent-driven or automated workflows that make decisions based on user input, data, or context.
• Coordinate and settle value on Arc with USDC using Circle infrastructure (e.g. Circle Wallets, Circle CCTP/Bridge Kit, Circle Gateway) as part of your commerce flow (subscriptions, usage-based APIs, tipping, marketplace payments, etc.).
Recommended Gemini Models :
Participants are encouraged to select the Gemini model that best fits their agent use case:
Gemini 3 Flash
Recommended for transactional and payment agents . Optimized for speed and low latency , making it ideal for real-time commerce flows such as checkout, payment execution, balance checks, and on-chain confirmations.
Gemini 3 Pro
Designed for advanced reasoning and “Deep Think” capabilities . Ideal for agents that need to negotiate prices, analyze complex transaction histories, manage treasuries, or coordinate multi-step financial workflows .
Multimodal Commerce Examples:
Gemini’s multimodal capabilities enable new forms of agent-driven commerce, such as:
• Using vision models to verify physical goods or delivery proof before releasing a USDC payment
• Analyzing invoice or receipt images to automate settlement, reconciliation, or refunds on Arc
